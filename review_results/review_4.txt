The work is technically strong because it has a thorough experimental design that includes warm-up phases, isolated Dockerized environments, and measurements of write costs from start to finish. Architects can use the data to figure out where the crossover points are. For example, it shows that PG is 1.4 to 8 times faster for ingestion through 1440p, while PM gets better at 4K and 5K. The study also measures storage efficiency, showing that PG has a 4% overhead because of TOAST and page-allocation, while PM has almost no overhead. The authors recognize potential threats to validity, including the single-host configuration and absence of network latency; nevertheless, the study is profoundly informative. Future research investigating distributed deployments or PostgreSQL "Large Objects" (LO) would significantly augment this study. In general, the paper shows that the choice between inline and externalized storage is not the same for everyone; it depends a lot on the size of the payload and the focus of the workload.
